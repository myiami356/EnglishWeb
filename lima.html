Micro-grammar is often confused, but important!

In learning English, sometimes we are confused by small grammar or micro-grammar such as "a little vs little", "say vs tell", or "much vs many". Although it seems trivial, these small differences can change the meaning and affect the clarity of the message we want to convey.

Below is a practical explanation and example sentences that can help you understand these differences easily. Come on, understand and practice to become even better!

Grammar Pairs Explanation Example Sentences
A little vs Little A little: little but enough
Little: little and not enough 		I have a little money left, so I can buy snacks.
He has little time to finish the project.
Much vs Many Much: for uncountable objects
Many: for countable objects 		I don‚Äôt have much sugar.

Say vs Tell Say: no need for person object
Tell: must mention who is being told 		She said it was raining.
He told me to wait outside.
